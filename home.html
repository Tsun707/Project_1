Balkans

The area is located in the south of Europe made up of 11 countries, about 550,000 km2 and 55,000,000 populations. Due to the historical reasons, the place becomes an area where multiple cultures mix, and somehow unsteadiness.

Greece:

Greece, a nation with a rich cultural heritage, is known for its ancient history and contributions to Western civilization. Greek culture is characterized by its emphasis on philosophy, art, and literature. Traditional Greek music, dance, and cuisine, with dishes like moussaka and souvlaki, are deeply rooted in their culture, reflecting a love for music, dance, and delicious Mediterranean flavors.

Serbia:

Serbia, located in the heart of the Balkans, boasts a diverse culture influenced by its turbulent history and varied influences. Serbian culture places a strong emphasis on family, hospitality, and traditional folk music, known as "tamburica. " Additionally, their cuisine features hearty dishes such as cevapi and sarma, showcasing a blend of Slavic and Mediterranean flavors.

Croatia:

Croatia, nestled along the Adriatic coast, showcases a unique cultural blend influenced by its historical ties to Italy, Austria, and the Balkans. Croatian culture is celebrated through festivals, such as the Dubrovnik Summer Festival, which highlights music, theater, and dance. Their cuisine is marked by fresh seafood, pasta dishes, and world-renowned wines like Plavac Mali.

Albania:

Albania, a country with a diverse cultural heritage, is known for its unique blend of Illyrian, Greek, Roman, and Ottoman influences. Albanian culture is deeply rooted in hospitality, with guests often welcomed warmly. Traditional music, especially polyphonic singing, holds a special place in their cultural identity. Albanian cuisine features dishes like burek and tave kosi, reflecting the nation's Mediterranean and Balkan influences.

Bulgaria:

Bulgaria, situated in Southeastern Europe, boasts a rich cultural heritage influenced by Thracian, Slavic, and Ottoman traditions. Bulgarian culture is celebrated through vibrant folk festivals with traditional dance and music, like the horo dance. The cuisine features dishes such as banitsa and kebapche, offering a taste of Balkan and Mediterranean flavors.

Bosnia and Herzegovina:
